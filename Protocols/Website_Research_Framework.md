# Website Research Framework
**Purpose:** Generic template for researching websites across any industry/context
**Method:** 5-question analysis using competitor research
**Output:** Actionable insights for strategy, design, content, or positioning

---

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Competitor Research
Use Firecrawl MCP or manual research to identify top 5 performing websites in your category/location:
- Sort by: Google reviews, SEO ranking, engagement metrics, conversion signals
- Document: URL, key features, design approach, messaging

### Step 2: Answer the 5 Questions
For your own project, answer each question based on what top performers do and your specific context.

---

## THE 5 QUESTIONS

### Question 1: Who is this website for? (The visitor, not the business)
**Why:** Understanding your actual visitor (not your business) determines everything — messaging, layout, trust signals, copy tone.

**What to research:** 
- Who visits top competitor sites?
- What problem are they solving?
- What's their job title, age range, technical level?
- What are they searching for when they land?

**Your Answer:**
```
[Who is the actual visitor and what do they care about right now?]
```

### Question 2: What's the ONE action you want them to take?
**Why:** Every website has a primary CTA. Everything else supports that one decision.

**Common CTAs:**
- Book a demo
- Join a waitlist
- Buy now
- Request a quote
- Call now
- See live example
- Download resource
- Schedule consultation

**Your Answer:**
```
[Primary CTA: ________________]
[Why this CTA matters: ________________]
```

### Question 3: What objections does the visitor have?
**Why:** Visitors have reasons to leave. Your site structure should address their specific doubts.

**Common objections → Sections needed:**

| Objection | Section Required | Your Context |
|:---|:---|:---|
| "I don't trust this company" | Testimonials, logos, case studies, credentials | — |
| "I don't understand the product" | How it works, demo video, visual explainer, tutorials | — |
| "Is this worth the price?" | Pricing breakdown, ROI calculator, comparison table | — |
| "Is this right for me specifically?" | Use cases, audience segmentation, industry-specific messaging | — |
| "Will this actually work?" | Results/metrics, before/after, guarantees, risk reversal | — |
| "Is this company legit?" | About page, team bios, awards, press mentions | — |

**Your Objection Analysis:**
```
[What would make someone leave without taking action?]
[Which sections do you need to address these doubts?]
```

---

### Question 4: What's the vibe?
**Why:** Vibe determines visual design, copy tone, imagery, layout spacing. It's the "feeling" the site creates.

**Vibe options:**

| Vibe | Visual Characteristics | When to use |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Premium | Dark backgrounds, lots of whitespace, minimal text, luxury feel | High-end, expensive, exclusive products |
| Playful | Bright colors, rounded shapes, casual tone, friendly emoji/illustrations | Consumer apps, young audience, accessible tone |
| Technical | Data-forward, clean grids, monospace fonts, credibility signals | B2B SaaS, engineering, finance, security |
| Bold | Large typography, high contrast, full-bleed images, confident | Startups, disruptive brands, confident positioning |
| Minimal | Maximum whitespace, 1–2 accent colors, focus on clarity | Premium/luxury, information-heavy, clarity-focused |
| Professional | Structured layout, business fonts, corporate imagery | B2B services, consulting, established brands |
| Warm | Approachable, human imagery, softer colors, friendly | Community, health, education, personal services |

**Your Vibe:**
```
[Pick one primary vibe: ____________]
[What does that vibe look/feel like for your site? ________________]
[Why is this the right vibe for your visitor? ________________]
```

### Question 5: Do you have existing brand assets?
**Why:** Consistency matters. Starting from scratch vs. having a guide changes everything about execution.

**Options:**
- **Yes** — brand guide/style system exists
- **Partial** — some assets (colors, fonts) exist but need consistency
- **No** — starting from scratch, building brand as you go

**Your Answer:**
```
[Yes / Partial / No]

If yes/partial, document:
- Primary color: ____________
- Secondary color(s): ____________
- Font(s): ____________
- Logo: ____________
- Tone of voice: ____________
- Imagery style: ____________

If no:
[What brand identity do you want to create? ________________]
[What should it communicate about you? ________________]
```

## NEXT STEPS

Once this research is complete, use your answers to:
- **Design brief** → Feed into design tool (Claude Design, Figma, Stitch, etc.)
- **Content strategy** → Outline copy, messaging hierarchy, section content
- **Information architecture** → Map page structure and navigation
- **Competitor differentiation** → Identify what makes your approach unique
- **Success metrics** → Define what "working" looks like for each section
