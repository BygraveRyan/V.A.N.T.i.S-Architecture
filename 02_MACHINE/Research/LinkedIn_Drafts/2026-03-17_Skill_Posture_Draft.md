### Draft Content

Most Data Engineers have a "low market match" score and they don't even know it.

The problem? Most professionals treat their career as a static list of tools rather than a dynamic **Skill Posture**. 

I learned this the hard way while diving into **SCD Type 2** and **Star Schemas** recently. 

It’s easy to learn the *definitions* (the plumbing). But the real challenge is architecting for **Market Gravity**.

In a market where technical gravity shifts every 2-3 years, your legacy ETL expertise doesn’t just "age"—it decays. 

I've been building a **Skill Posture Analysis** framework to map real-time market demand against my own stack.

Here’s what I found:
🔹 **Legacy Posture**: High focus on 'How to code it.'
🔹 **Modern Posture**: High focus on 'Why this structure.'
🔹 **AI-Native Posture**: High focus on 'How to govern it.'

If your current posture is still built on "knowing tools" rather than "designing architectures," you're building on shifting sand.

The path to seniority isn't just knowing *when* to use SCD Type 2. It's knowing *why* you're still using it in an AI-Native world.

**Question for the DE community:** Are you auditing the "Market Gravity" of your tech stack, or just adding another tool to the list? 👇
