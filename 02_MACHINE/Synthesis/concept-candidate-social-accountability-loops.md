# Concept Candidate

## Name
concept-social-accountability-loops

## Explanation
Social accountability loops are design patterns in social and productivity applications that leverage peer comparison and community progress to drive individual habits. By visualizing friends' progress alongside one's own, users are more likely to stay committed to a goal.

## Why It Matters
It creates a sense of shared responsibility and healthy competition, which is often more effective than individual self-discipline for maintaining long-term habits (like exercise or learning).

## Example
The "Team Activity Goal App" idea, where users can see their friends' and colleagues' daily steps and progress towards a shared goal, similar to Apple Watch "rings" or Strava activity feeds.
