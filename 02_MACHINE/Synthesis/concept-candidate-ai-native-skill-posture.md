# AI-Native Skill Posture

## Definition
An evolution of [[concept-career-skill-posture-analysis]], specifically focused on the alignment of a professional's expertise with the shift toward **AI-Native Engineering**. It measures the ability to transition from "Manual Contributor" to "Agentic Orchestrator," identifying the specific technical and cognitive skills required to lead AI-driven development cycles.

## Core Principles
1. **Orchestration vs. Execution**: Traditional "Skill Posture" values the ability to write code; "AI-Native Posture" values the ability to *direct* agents to write, test, and deploy code.
2. **Context Engineering**: The high-ROI skill of the future is the ability to maintain, curate, and inject high-quality context (like the VANTIS Galaxy) into agentic workflows.
3. **Verification Velocity**: As AI increases the speed of output, the professional's value shifts toward their **Verification Posture**—the speed and accuracy with which they can validate AI-generated architectures.
4. **Agentic Troubleshooting**: The ability to diagnose failures in autonomous loops (e.g., identifying "hallucinations" in an RAG pipeline or "drift" in a prompt).

## Why It Matters
As [[concept-ai-native-engineering]] becomes the industry standard, professionals with a "Legacy Posture" (purely manual execution) will face high decay rates. This framework identifies the **Minimum Viable Orchestration** (MVO) skills needed to stay at the forefront of the market gravity.

## Practical Application
For a Data Engineer, an "AI-Native Posture" means moving from "Writing SQL Queries" to "Building Agentic SQL-Generation Pipelines" and "Governing Metadata for LLM Consumption."
